About Endings/Beginnings

The 24th Annual Massachusetts Multicultural Film Festival is pleased to present its spring season, “Endings/Beginnings.” Whether art or entertainment, documentary or fiction, movies experiment with--and at times manipulate-- our sense of reality. It is often through the process of entering into a film and emerging from it that the shock of a cultural experience is most apparent. Embracing a range of genres and styles--from psychological drama to black comedy, from performance to animation, and fiction to non-fiction, while also exploring experimental, observational and highly stylized technique--our screenings interrogate the ways movies begin and end. Each screening or event foregrounds not only the role of titles, credits and opening/closing sequences, but also narrative elements that may be considered ‘answers’ to the ‘questions’ of the openings. Our program proposes that viewers’ openness to cinema’s surprising moments lead us to unexpected places that unlock their secrets to us. Beginning with..., moving through...and ending with..., the festival itself queries the idea of closure in the classical Hollywood narrative in a current climate of multimedia ‘binge-watching,’ sequelization and adaptations.
