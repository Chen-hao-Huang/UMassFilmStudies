Gynocine Conference - Oct 27-29

In this conference instead of employing the controversial term "cine femenino" we will use the neologism "gynocine." The aim is to establish a bridge between (primarily Anglo Saxon) feminist film theory and the cinematographic practices of Spanish women filmmakers, in order to establish a new dialog.

"GYNOCINE: Mujeres, Dones and Cinema Conference" will center around the production of Spanish women cineastes and will focus on: the body and sexuality; violence and representations of violence; lesbianism and queer theory; imagology and otherness; motherhood and pregnancy; family relationships; couples; and auteurism, among other topics.

With the participation of:
Margarita Alexandre (actress, director, producer), Judith Colell (director) Mar Coll (director), Inés París (director, screenwriter), Alicia Luna (screenwriter), Margarita Lobo (Filmoteca Nacional), Susan Martin-Márquez (Rutgers University), Núria Triana-Toribio (University of Manchester), Kathleen Vernon (SUNY at Stony Brook). Massachusetts Multicultural Film Festival - Spring 2011

The 18th Annual Massachusetts Multicultural Film Festival, "Migrations,"highlights interconnections among cinema, migration, war and memory in the context of contemporary cultural and ethno-national landscapes, screening award-winning documentary and feature films from South Africa, Bosnia, Russia, Israel, Mauritius, Iran, Palestine, Spain, Mexico and the U.S. From February 2 to April 27, this season explores the primordial role played by filmmakers working in partnership beyond and across borders, whether geographical, psychological, socio-economic, cultural or historical. All events are free and open to the public. Watch the WGBY interview with Catherine Portuges, Director of Film Studies at UMass and Curator of the Massachusetts Multicultural Film Festival. [More...]

 

DEFA Summer Film Institute - July 17-22

Held in the beautiful Pioneer Valley of western Massachusetts, the DEFA Film Library’s biennial Summer Film Institutes provide an opportunity for scholars to spend a week together in a relaxed setting watching movies and discussing East German cinema from a variety of perspectives. As a complement to our 2007 Film Institute, in summer 2011 we will explore the filmic relationships between East Germany and the so-called “Third World” by focusing on Asia, Africa and the Middle East. The Institute will be accompanied by screenings of a wide range of East German film materials, including feature films, documentaries, children’s films, newsreels, and Foreign Ministry films. Keynote Speaker: Young-Sun Hong (SUNY Stony Brook) “East Germany's Third World.” [More...]

2011 Five College Student Film & Video Festival

The 2011 Five College Student Film & Video Festival will take place Friday, February 25, 2011 at 7:00 p.m. in the Main Lecture Hall at Hampshire College. Twelve cash prizes will be awarded to the top ranked entries for each category, the top winner from each of the Five Colleges and the grand prize winner of the festival. Pioneer Valley Jewish Film Festival - 2011

The PBJFF celebrates its 6th annual valleywide festival of global, award-winning films. This year's theme "Caring Across Borders." Whether it's daring to care about the "enemy" (Surviving Hitler: A Love Story, For My Father, My So-Called Enemy), showing kindness to strangers (Anita, No. 4 Street of our Lady), or venturing into new cultural and spiritual territory (Srugim, Off and Running, Circumcise Me) this year's characters find meaning as they forge cross-cultural connections. They want to belong to a community and they also want the freedom to connect with people and experiences that are new and unfamiliar. [More...]
